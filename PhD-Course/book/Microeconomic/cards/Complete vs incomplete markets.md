# Complete vs incomplete markets

Main note: [Microeconomic/02_Choice_under_Uncertainty_and_State_Contingent_Equilibrium](../Microeconomic/02_Choice_under_Uncertainty_and_State_Contingent_Equilibrium)

Markets are complete if

$$
\operatorname{rank}(A)=S.
$$

Then every state-contingent payoff is spanned and priced uniquely:

$$
\pi(d)=\psi^\top d.
$$

If markets are incomplete, some risks cannot be traded; state prices may be nonunique; equilibrium may be constrained inefficient relative to Arrow-Debreu complete markets.
