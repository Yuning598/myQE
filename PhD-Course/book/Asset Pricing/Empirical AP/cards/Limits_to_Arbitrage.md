# Limits to Arbitrage

Source: EF8083 slides, pp. 363-390  
Backlinks: [08_Limits_to_Arbitrage_Prospect_Theory](../08_Limits_to_Arbitrage_Prospect_Theory) | [07_Behavioral_Empirical_AP_Overview](../07_Behavioral_Empirical_AP_Overview)

Mispricing may persist because arbitrage is risky and constrained.

Arbitrage return example:
\[
E[R]=pm-(1-p)L.
\]

Even if \(E[R]>0\), arbitrage may be impossible if downside loss \(L\) exceeds capital or margin capacity \(K\):
\[
L>K.
\]

Empirical proxies:

- short interest / borrow fee;
- idiosyncratic volatility;
- institutional ownership;
- fund flow pressure;
- leverage and funding liquidity.
