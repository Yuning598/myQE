# Prospect Theory and Lottery Preference

Source: EF8083 slides, pp. 391-420  
Backlinks: [08_Limits_to_Arbitrage_Prospect_Theory](../08_Limits_to_Arbitrage_Prospect_Theory)

Prospect value:
\[
v(x)=
\begin{cases}
x^\alpha,&x\ge0,\\
-\lambda(-x)^\alpha,&x<0,
\end{cases}
\qquad \lambda>1.
\]

Lottery preference: overweighting small probabilities of large gains creates demand for high-skewness assets.

If price includes lottery premium:
\[
P=P^*+\pi_{lot},
\]
then
\[
E[R]=\frac{E[X]}{P^*+\pi_{lot}}
<
\frac{E[X]}{P^*}.
\]

Prediction: high idiosyncratic skewness / lottery-like stocks have low subsequent average returns.
